Privacy Policy — Mathéquête

Application: Mathéquête
Android identifier: com.coresrdi.mathequete
Publisher: Jeff Gravel (Quebec, Canada)
Brand: coresrdi
Effective date: May 12, 2026
Last updated: May 12, 2026

One-sentence summary: Mathéquête is an educational game for children that runs entirely on the device. We do not collect, store, or transmit any personal data to our servers.

1. Who we are

Mathéquête is developed by Jeff Gravel, an independent developer based in Quebec, Canada, under the coresrdi brand. This document describes the privacy practices applicable to the Mathéquête mobile application distributed on the Google Play Store under the identifier com.coresrdi.mathequete.

2. Target audience — children's application

Mathéquête is designed specifically for children aged 4 to 12. We strictly comply with:

  • the Children's Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA) (United States);
  • the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) (European Union), including special provisions for minors (GDPR-K);
  • Quebec's Law 25 (Act respecting the protection of personal information in the private sector);
  • Google Play's Families Policy.

As a result: no targeted advertising, no advertising identifier, no behavioral analytics, no sharing with third parties for marketing purposes.

3. Data the application collects

3.1 No data transmitted to our servers

Mathéquête runs entirely locally on the device. The only data generated by gameplay (child profiles, progression, scores, character choices, gameplay statistics) is stored exclusively in the device's internal memory. It never leaves the device and is never sent to coresrdi or any third party.

3.2 Data stored locally

The application records on the device:

  • child profiles created by the parent (first name only, optional);
  • game progression (completed levels, captured enemies, rewards);
  • preferences (language, sound, character choice, skins);
  • Premium purchase status (synchronized with Google Play Billing, see section 5).

This data is encrypted locally using AES-256-CBC with HMAC-SHA256 authentication. It can be erased at any time from the "Manage Profiles" screen of the game, or by uninstalling the app.

3.3 No advertising identifier

Mathéquête does not use the Android advertising identifier (AAID / com.google.android.gms.permission.AD_ID) and does not read any persistent hardware identifier (IMEI, MAC, etc.).

4. Android permissions requested

4.1 CAMERA permission (android.permission.CAMERA)

Exclusive use: The application uses the device's camera only to scan QR codes locally. No image, video, or video stream is recorded, stored, or transmitted. The content read from the QR code is processed locally and not sent to a server.

The camera permission is requested at the moment the child activates the QR scan feature (not when the app starts). It can be denied or revoked at any time from Android settings, without preventing the rest of the game from working.

4.2 INTERNET permission

The application requests Internet permission solely to communicate with Google Play Billing servers (in-app purchase validation). No other network communication is performed by Mathéquête.

5. In-app purchases (Google Play Billing)

Mathéquête offers a single non-consumable purchase: Mathéquête Premium (product ID: mathequete_premium). This unlocks additional content in the game.

Purchase transactions:

  • are handled exclusively by Google Play Billing;
  • we never see payment information (credit card, etc.);
  • only a purchase confirmation token transits, verified locally on the device;
  • per Play Families policy, any purchase must be authorized by a parent via Google Play parental controls.

6. Sharing with third parties

We do not sell, rent, or share any user data with third parties. The only technically involved third party is:

  • Google Play Services — for app distribution and in-app purchase processing. See the Google privacy policy.

No third-party analytics SDK (Google Analytics, Firebase Analytics, Facebook SDK, Unity Ads, AdMob, etc.) is integrated into the application.

7. Security

Data stored locally on the device is encrypted (AES-256-CBC + HMAC-SHA256). Any network communication performed by the application (only to Google Play Billing) is protected by TLS / HTTPS. Since no data leaves the device toward our servers, the risk of a data breach on our side is zero.

8. User and parent rights

Pursuant to GDPR, Quebec's Law 25, and COPPA, you (and the legal parent/guardian of a child user) have the following rights:

  • Right of access: all game data is visible on the "Manage Profiles" screen.
  • Right of rectification: profiles, names, and preferences can be modified at any time in the game.
  • Right of erasure: delete a profile in "Manage Profiles", or uninstall the app to erase everything.
  • Right of portability: on request to the email below, we explain how to export local data.
  • Right to object: since no data is processed for profiling, this right is automatically respected.

To exercise these rights or ask any privacy-related question:

Contact email: coresrdi@gmail.com
Response time: within 30 days (15 days for GDPR requests).

9. Data retention

Data stored locally is retained as long as the application is installed on the device. It is automatically deleted:

  • when a child profile is manually deleted;
  • when the application is uninstalled;
  • when data is cleared via Android settings (Apps → Mathéquête → Storage → Clear data).

10. International transfers

No international transfer of user data is performed by Mathéquête, since no data leaves the user's device. The only communications (toward Google Play Billing) are governed by Google's policy.

11. Changes to this policy

We may update this privacy policy, in particular to reflect legal changes or app evolutions. The "last updated" date at the top of the document indicates the current version. The full change history is public on our GitHub repository.

Any substantial change will be communicated to users via an app update or a message displayed at game launch.

12. Governing law

This policy is governed by the laws of the Province of Quebec, Canada. Any dispute related to this policy shall be submitted to the jurisdiction of the competent courts of Quebec.
